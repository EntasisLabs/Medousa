# Memory & identity

**Audience:** Medousa app users who want her to remember *who they are* — not
just the last chat.

Medousa keeps two related pictures:

| Layer | What it is |
|-------|------------|
| **Memory** | Texture of what happened — chats, projects, long threads |
| **Identity** | Essentials about *you* — preferences, people, rhythms |

Essentials can surface at the start of a turn. You can teach one fact at a time
or export/edit the picture as markdown.

---

## Settings → Memory

Open **Settings → Memory** to tune how long chats stay vivid, retention-style
charter options, and related rules. Exact controls evolve; the intent is: keep
what matters without drowning every turn in noise.

---

## Teach her who you are

In chat:

- *“Remember that Mario is my brother.”*
- *“I prefer concise answers unless I ask for depth.”*
- *“Call this profile work — shorter, more formal.”*

**Profiles** are hats on **one** brain (`work`, `home`, …) — not separate
accounts. Switch profile when the context changes; workshops are *which engine*,
profiles are *which hat* on that engine.

---

## Export and edit

Ask Medousa to export your identity / memory picture as markdown when you want
to review or edit offline. Hand an edited version back when you’re ready.

Power users can also use CLI identity helpers — see
[CLI & workspace](../cookbook/cli-and-workspace.md).

---

## What she won’t silently do

- She shouldn’t invent lasting facts you didn’t teach or that weren’t grounded
  in your history.
- Tool and channel actions can still ask before acting — see **Settings → Reach**.
